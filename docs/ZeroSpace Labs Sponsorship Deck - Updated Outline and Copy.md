## Executive Breakdown

The current deck is an 18-section Reveal.js presentation. It keeps the same core sponsorship story as the older outline but has been redesigned into a more visual, image-led sales deck.

Narrative arc:

1. Establish ZeroSpace Labs as NYC's creative technology event platform.
2. Prove growth and reach with updated aggregate metrics.
3. Show that the venue itself is a sponsor asset.
4. Define the audience and the companies already represented in the room.
5. Break out four event series as sponsor surfaces.
6. Package the sponsor value around content, direct access, livestream, and immersive venue integrations.
7. Present series-level and a la carte pricing.
8. Close with social proof and a direct partnership ask.

Major updates from the older outline:

- Aggregate stats increased from 3,500+ registrations / 3,100+ approved attendees / 15 events to 5,000+ registrations / 4,200+ approved attendees / 30+ events.
- The measured reporting window changed from May 2025-February 2026 to December 2024-February 2026.
- The Venue moved earlier, before "Who's in the Room" and "The Audience," making the physical production studio a primary proof point.
- ComfyUI NYC changed from 9 events and counting to 14 events and counting, with 13x growth from 33 approved in December 2024 to 425 in January 2026.
- DATLAB NYC changed from 4 events to 5 events, with event-level growth from 184 approved at Event X to 266 at Event XIV.
- Gen AI Film Jam and Vibe Coding Competition are positioned as monthly in the deck, while the older outline positioned them as quarterly.
- Sponsorship pricing is split into two slides: series-level partnerships and a la carte / competition pricing.
- The appendix target sponsor list from the older outline is not included in the current deck.

Open copy/data checks:

- DATLAB is described as "250+ approved per event," but the growth chart shows five events at 184, 193, 181, 230, and 266 approved attendees. If "250+" means current run-rate rather than average, consider making that explicit.
- The closing CTA says "10x our growth in 2026." The prior outline used "plans to take key formats to other cities." Confirm whether "10x" is the intended forward-looking claim.
- Film Jam and Vibe Coding are marked monthly in the deck. Confirm this cadence before sending externally.
