# Creative AI NYC Sponsorship Deck
## Outline, Copy, and Sponsor Strategy

Source deck: `creative-ai-nyc/index.html`

This document is the source-of-truth copy outline for the Creative AI NYC sponsorship deck. The public deck positions Creative AI NYC as a broader monthly creative AI event from Hypercentric.

---

## Executive Summary

Creative AI NYC is the evolution of the Hypercentric community event platform into a broader monthly event for hybrid creative AI workflows.

ComfyUI NYC is treated as the origin story and credibility proof, not the ongoing event brand. The deck uses ComfyUI NYC growth and audience data to show that Hypercentric has already assembled a trusted in-person community for technical creators, AI artists, founders, and production operators.

Sponsor thesis:

- Creators are moving from single-tool workflows to hybrid stacks.
- The future is commercial models plus open-source workflows plus creative-tool platforms plus repair and finishing techniques.
- Sponsors need more than logo placement; they need live adoption, workflow education, qualitative feedback, recorded content, and community authority.

Current sponsor deliverables include talks, demos, demo surfaces, livestream/recording, edited videos, clips, recap assets, stage visibility, and qualitative feedback where available.

Opt-in sponsor lists are a planned future capability and must not be sold as current inventory until the consent and collection process exists.

## SLIDE 3: ORIGIN STORY

**ComfyUI NYC proved the demand. Creative AI NYC broadens the stage.**

The open-source workflow community gave us the first clear signal: creators want to gather in person to understand how AI systems actually work.

ComfyUI NYC is now the credibility proof, not the endpoint. Creative AI NYC expands that energy into a broader event for hybrid production, where artists and technical operators use the best available model, tool, or workflow for the shot in front of them.

Proof points:

## SLIDE 4: WHY NOW

**The landscape is shifting from model demos to production systems.**

Creators are not choosing one AI product for every problem. They are assembling context, testing models, repairing artifacts, and moving between commercial and open-source systems depending on the project.

Supporting ideas:

- Model choice: creators choose commercial, open-source, image, video, audio, and multimodal models based on the creative constraint.
- Legible context: the new craft is compiling references, prompts, footage, assets, and constraints so models can produce usable output.
- Repair culture: production work depends on iteration, cleanup, relighting, upscaling, continuity fixes, and editorial judgment.

Purpose:

- Explain why model companies and tool platforms should care now.
- Tie the event to how creators are actually adapting.
